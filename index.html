How do dental implants work, and what is the implantation process like?

Dental implants are artificial tooth roots that are surgically inserted into the jawbone to give support for replacement teeth. The implantation process begins with a small incision being made in the gums, followed by the placement of the titanium root into the jawbone. Once the source is in place, an abutment (connector piece) is placed on top of it, and the replacement tooth is then attached to the abutment. Dental implants are a robust and stable way to support replacement teeth, and they can last for many years with proper care. Talk to a qualified implant dentist about your specific circumstances if you’re thinking about dental implants.

What should you expect after getting dental implants installed in your mouth?

Dental implants are a fantastic method to enhance your smile and confidence. Here’s what you can anticipate after getting dental implants. It’s normal to feel some soreness or discomfort in your gums following the initial placement of your implant. Within a few days, this should be gone. You might have some gum swelling, which should disappear within a week. Although it is typical, you may observe some gum bruising which will fade with time.

It is essential to keep your mouth clean after getting dental implants. This means brushing and flossing regularly and using an antibacterial mouthwash. Your dentist may also recommend using a special toothbrush or flossing tool to help keep your implants clean. You should also avoid eating hard or crunchy foods for weeks after getting your implants. This is to protect your gums and allow them to heal correctly. After a few weeks, you can slowly start introducing these foods back into your diet.
